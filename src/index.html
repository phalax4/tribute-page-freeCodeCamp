Yellow Stone National Park

Timeline Tribute

  • First Humans

    11,000 years ago via Land

    Native American tribes such as the Shoshone and Crow tribes making up the Clovis Culture setteled the valley.

  • Survey Expedition

    1869 & 1870 via Land

    A privately funded expedition called the Cook–Folsom–Peterson Expedition explored the Yellow Stone region.

    The Washburn-Langford-Doane Expedition formally surveyed the valley and was part of a larger movement to preserve the valley as a national park.

  • Recognition

    1872 via Congress

    The valley with Yellow Stone River was declared a National Park. Formal recognition that the area was a national treasure and landmark.

  • Native American Clashes

    1879 via Manifest Destiny

    Manifest Destiny heralded the westward expansion of American pioneers. With this came pushing out the Native Americans like the Shoshone further to the West. The Indian Sheepeater War and various other skirmishes caused minor clashes in Yellow Stone.

  • Early Conservation

    1880 via Park Services

    Poaching was rampant in the park as access became better and the area was made known to the wider continental US. Early gamekeepers tried to stop poaching of buffalo but to no avail.

  • Regaining Control

    1916 via Theodore Roosevelt and U.S. Army

    The U.S. Army built a fort and both regulated and policed the park while letting the public also have access. Theodore Roosevelt Park Protection Act further stablilized the park's backing with the National Park Service agency.

    National Park Service History of America

  • The New Deal

    1933-1942 via Franklin D. Roosevelt

    With the Great Depression, public works became a popular way for the government to reinvograte the economy. The Civilian Conservation Corps was a government relief agency that assigned men to develop many new facilities in Yellow Stone as part of the New Deal.

  • Tourism

    1950's via National Park Service

    Tourism was greatly revived and promotoed through the new network of national highways criss-crossing the country. This made getting to Yellow Stone far more easy. Mission 66 transformed Yellow Stone into an appealing and accessible destination both from outside and within the park grounds.

  • Nature Demands

    1959 via Mother Nature

    A series of massive wild forest fires slowed down the development of the park facilities. The Hebgen Lake erthquake revealed just how fragile human infastructure is in the face of nature. The new steaming hot geysers is a reminder of the fierce flames that live just beneath the surface in Yellow Stone.
